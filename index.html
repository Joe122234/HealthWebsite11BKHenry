Causes & Consequences

Why fitness social media becomes harmful

The issue arises primarily from the unregulated nature of social media content creation. Anyone can post fitness advice regardless of qualifications, often prioritizing popularity, aesthetics, or engagement over accuracy. Algorithms amplify visually appealing or extreme content, such as dramatic transformations or intense workouts, which can distort perceptions of health and fitness.

Prevention

Stay informed and intentional.

Preventing harm requires a balanced and educational approach rather than discouraging social media use entirely.

Prevention strategies

Media literacy

Media literacy skills should be taught to users so they can assess the reliability of fitness content, spot false information, and challenge false representations of bodies and performance.

Encourage diverse content

Encouraging exposure to diverse content that represents different body types, abilities, and fitness levels helps shift the focus from appearance to health and functionality.

Supportive communities

Fostering supportive online communities that emphasize well-being, progress, and inclusivity can reduce harmful comparisons and promote healthier engagement.

Spend less time online

Spend less time on social media to reduce exposure to harmful content.

Avoid unreliable content

Avoid negative and unrealistic content that may cause mental harm (comparison, anxiety, depression) and physical harm (taking fitness advice from unreliable sources).

Educate & verify

Educate yourself about sports and check multiple sources of information. Verify fitness information to prevent injury (for example, trying a new machine at the gym without knowing the right technique and getting hurt as a result).

Think critically

Think critically and incorporate asking questions when watching content. Use critical thinking tools to determine whether the source is reliable to prevent misinformation.

The goal

Critically assess the online content and recognize portrayals of bodies. Approach information with a critical eye. The primary goal is to empower individuals to critically assess online fitness content and recognize how bodies and exercise are portrayed on social media. This includes approaching information with a critical eye, valuing health and functionality over appearance, and understanding that fitness looks different for everyone.

By promoting thoughtful consumption of content and encouraging diversity, the goal is to support positive physical and mental health outcomes while maintaining the motivational benefits of social media. Encourage the consumption of diverse content that showcases different body types and fitness levels, and appreciate the value of health over appearance. Foster supportive online communities.

Practical steps

  • Limit daily social media use to reduce constant comparison and information overload.
  • Seek exercise knowledge from qualified professionals such as coaches, trainers, or healthcare providers.
  • Focus on the functional benefits of exercise rather than aesthetic outcomes.
  • Be sensible about intensity, recovery, and individual needs to prevent injury and burnout.

When you see a fitness video, ask:

Who?

Who is the producer of that source? Are they legitimate, reliable, and credible?

What?

What are they promoting? Are they promoting genuine fitness advice or just themselves and their bodies?

Why?

What is the motive behind the video? Does the person uploading the video gain something by you going to the gym after seeing this, messaging them, or gaining followers?

Take Action

Be Conscious. Be Critical. Be Informed.

Our campaign targets individuals who are active on social media and particularly vulnerable to body image pressure and misinformation. This audience is at a critical stage of physical development and identity formation, making accurate information and positive messaging especially important.

Key Message

Social media can have productive and positive content, but it is important to be conscious of misinformation regarding sports activities that could potentially harm both the physical and mental health of a person.

Call to Action

Learn to differentiate between professional and unprofessional fitness advice, and always look and verify information on social media through multiple reliable sources.
